OPERATING AGREEMENT FOR CAPITOL COWBOYS LLC (FULL TEXT)

Article I: Legal Authority

Capitol Cowboys is chartered as a limited liability, for profit corporation under and by virtue of the laws of the District of Columbia.

Article II: Name and Location

Section 1: Official Name - Capitol Cowboys
Section 2: Assumed Name - For purposes of advertising and promotion, the organization may at times utilize the assumed name of "The Cowboys" or simply "Cowboys." However, the official name shall remain the binding corporate name to transact business.
Section 3: Location - The principal office of the organization and its registered agent shall be located in Washington, D.C. This designation may be changed from time to time only by official action of the Board of Directors with the filing of an appropriate certificate with the Department of Licensing and Consumer Protection of the applicable jurisdiction.

Article III: Purpose

Section 1: Mission - The mission of the Capitol Cowboys' is to cultivate a brotherhood of purpose-driven men committed to personal excellence, professional advancement, and meaningful social-impact. We promote social responsibility, professional growth, and strong moral foundations, through the sharpening of personal character, cultivation of strong networks, and the daily embodiment of disciplined leadership rooted in traditional values. Through intentional service, elevated social events, mentorship, and authentic camaraderie, we empower our fellow Cowboys to thrive in a fast-paced, competitive world, while remaining true to themselves.

Section 3: Events Schedule - The organization shall host social and recruiting events each year. The number and type of events shall be determined by the Board at its discretion. At the first meeting of each newly elected Board, a preliminary event schedule must be established. This schedule may be updated throughout the year if the Board determines, in its judgment, that a planned event is not feasible due to logistical, social, or financial reasons. The Board retains authority to adjust the event calendar accordingly.

Section 4: Restrictions - No activity of the organization shall support political campaigns on behalf of any candidate for public office. All members and directors must comply with a separate Code of Conduct adopted by the Board of Directors, which shall be reviewed and updated as necessary.

Article IV: Membership

Section 1: Definition of Active Status - A member, advisor or Director is considered active if they fulfill their responsibilities to the organization. For members, this includes maintaining good standing by paying dues and attending at least two main events and one recruiting event annually. For Advisers, this includes giving counsel and actively seeking opportunities to assist the board in the execution of their duties.

For Directors, active status also requires being responsive to communications, attending meetings, performing assigned duties, and attending at least three main events and two recruiting events per year.

Section 2: Eligibility - Any person expressing an interest in Capitol Cowboys' mission of community, opportunity, and service is eligible for membership. A member-in-good-standing shall have dues in a currently paid-up status as defined by the Board of Directors and must comply with the organization's Code of Conduct.

Section 3: Membership Fees - Membership shall be for a period of one year. The annual dues shall be $500, to be charged on the first of X each year. Categories of membership and any additional fees shall be established by the Board of Directors. All members, including those serving on the Board of Directors and advisory roles, are required to pay membership dues unless otherwise designated as members of the Cowboys' Founder Board.

Section 4: Transferability and Entitlements - Membership is not transferable and does not allow any rights or interest in the assets or income of the organization.

Section 5: Meeting Agenda - The Chair shall determine the agenda for each meeting. Meetings shall begin with attendance, followed by reports from each Board member on their respective responsibilities, including progress updates and any outstanding issues. This shall be followed by a presentation and discussion on resolutions. The Chair retains full discretion over which resolutions are formally included and presented for a vote. Initiatives intended for Board action must be introduced as resolutions. Resolutions are commented and discussed by the board and then subject to a 2/3rds vote to pass.

If the board disagrees with a vetoed resolution or agenda item by the Chair, they may override his veto with a 2/3rds vote.

Section 5: Cowboys Founder Board - The initial members of the Board of Directors shall transition to a special advisory group called the Cowboys Founder Board. Members shall have lifetime membership and be exempt from annual dues. The Cowboys Founder Board may veto any Board of Directors' action by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of all active members. This body upholds the original mission and values of Capitol Cowboys. Members may resign their position, and active members may nominate and approve new members. Inactive members may be removed by a 2/3rds vote of the active Cowboys Founder Board.

Article V: Board of Directors

Section 1: Governance - The business, property, programs, bylaws, Code of Conduct, and membership of the organization of this corporation shall be managed and controlled by the Board of Directors shall consist of seven (7) duly elected voting members. The Board of Directors retain those powers granted under the applicable state statutes which permit all necessary and convenient actions to affect all the approved purposes for Capitol Cowboys.

Section 2: Terms of Office - Directors elected at Annual Meetings shall serve for a term of up to two years, subject to reelection after the first year. After serving two consecutive years, a Director may not be reappointed or reelected to the Board. However, they may be appointed to serve as an advisor at the discretion of the Board. Each Director must maintain member-in-good-standing status.

Section 3: Vacancies - Vacancies shall be filled by the Board of Directors upon recommendation of the Chair for the remaining, unexpired term. Appointees serving less than two years are eligible for two additional full terms.

Section 4: Advisors - Any individual, including former Directors and Officers and outside individuals, may be appointed to serve as a nonvoting advisor for up to two consecutive fiscal years. Advisors may contribute to discussions and offer guidance but shall not have voting rights or formal authority over Board decisions.

Section 5: Attendance and Deliverables Rule - Directors with three consecutive, unexcused absences from Board or member meetings shall be removed from the Board.

Directors are also required to attend at least one main event and two recruiting events per year. Members are required to attend at least two main events and one recruiting event annually. Excused absences must be submitted in advance and are subject to approval by the Chair.

Repeated use of the same excuse or failure to provide a credible justification may render the absence unexcused at the Chair's discretion. Participation in meetings via video call is permitted, provided the member can hear and be heard clearly by all attendees.

Directors are also required to actively participate in projects for the group. Failure to be included in the planning and execution of the group's mission will result in removal subject to the procedures listed in Section 8.

Repeated use of the same excuse or failure to provide a credible justification may render the absence of work unexcused at the Chair's discretion.

Section 6: Compensation - Directors shall serve as volunteers and receive no compensation except reimbursement for certain approved expenses.

Section 7: Commitment and Ethics Rule - Each Director must maintain high ethical standards and commitment to Capitol Cowboys' mission. All Directors must comply with the organization's Code of Conduct. A single violation of the Code of Conduct or ethical standards may trigger a removal process, which shall include the initiation of procedures by the Internal Affairs Officer and a hearing composed of Board members designated by the Chair.

Section 8: Removal - Three violations of the attendance and deliverables policy shall trigger a removal process. Any Board member may bring a case against the offending member to initiate proceedings. Upon receiving notice of a case, the Chair shall set a date for a hearing and preside over it.

Shall the violation pertain to the Chair, the Vice Chair shall preside.

At the hearing, the bringing member shall present evidence of the violations. The Board, excluding the subject of the hearing, shall then vote, and removal or discipline shall require a two-thirds (4 votes) majority to pass.

Sanctions may include suspension, removal, or any other reasonable disciplinary measure the Board deems appropriate.

Section 9: Self-Removal - Service on the board is deemed to be voluntary and can be terminated at any time. In the case of self-termination, members will have no lingering responsibilities to the board.

A replacement shall be promptly found and approved by 2/3rds vote.

Article VI: Meetings

Section 1: Annual Meeting - An Annual Meeting of the organization and its members shall be held each year. The purpose of this meeting is to provide updates on upcoming events, finances, membership matters, and any other business deemed necessary by the Board of Directors.

Section 2: Meetings & Notice - Regular meetings of the Board shall be held monthly. Special meetings may be called with seven days' notice. Notice must be provided in writing and may be delivered by email, text message, or physical mail. The notice must include the date, time, location (or virtual link), and purpose of the meeting. Failure to provide complete notice may render the meeting invalid.

Section 3: Quorum & Voting - A majority of Directors present shall constitute a quorum. A simple majority vote is required for action. Directors may participate via video conference, provided they can hear and be heard by all participants. No proxy votes are allowed.

Section 4: Rules of Order - Robert's Rules of Order (current edition) shall govern all meetings.

Article VII: Officers

Section 1: Officers - The organization shall have the following officers: Chair, Vice Chair, Internal Affairs Officer, External Affairs Officer, Communications Officer, Social Officer, and Events Officer. The Vice Chair shall also perform the functions of Secretary and Treasurer.

Section 2: Election and Term of Office - Officers are elected annually by members-in-good-standing from among the Board of Directors. Officers may serve a maximum of two consecutive one-year terms. After serving two consecutive years, an officer may be appointed as an advisor but shall no longer be eligible to serve on the Board of Directors in any capacity.

Section 3: Duties of Board Chair - The Chair shall serve as the chief executive officer of the organization and provide overall leadership and strategic direction for Capitol Cowboys. The Chair shall preside at all meetings of the Board of Directors and of the membership, appoint all standing and special committees, serve as an ex officio member of all committees, and have the authority to call special meetings of the Board. The Chair shall oversee execution of the organization's programs and events, supervise the performance of officers and committee chairs, and act as the principal spokesperson for the organization. The Chair may delegate responsibilities to other officers or members as needed and shall have the authority to take emergency actions on behalf of the organization between meetings, subject to ratification by the Board. The Chair shall preside over meetings, appoint committees, and ensure oversight of corporate business.

Section 4: Duties of Vice Chair - The Vice Chair shall serve in the absence of the Chair and assume the Chair's duties when necessary. The Vice Chair shall coordinate the Annual Meeting and ensure accurate and timely recording of minutes for all Board and membership meetings. Additionally, the Vice Chair shall oversee the organization's financial operations, including the preparation of annual budgets, maintenance of financial records, and timely financial reporting to the Board. The Vice Chair shall also ensure financial transparency and accountability across all organizational expenditures and revenue streams.

Section 5: Duties of Internal Affairs Officer - The Internal Affairs Officer shall ensure internal compliance with organizational policies and procedures. They shall handle internal grievances, monitor member conduct in accordance with the Code of Conduct, support onboarding of new members, and oversee internal governance processes to promote organizational integrity and member accountability. They shall also advise the Events Chair on setting up and executing recruiting events.

Section 6: Duties of External Affairs Officer - The External Affairs Officer shall build and maintain strategic relationships with external partners to support Capitol Cowboys' goals in service, sponsorships, and business development. The External Affairs Officer shall proactively engage with sponsors, community leaders, and peer organizations to identify, develop, and sustain mutually beneficial collaborations that align with the organization's mission. They shall also represent the organization in external forums, advocate for strategic initiatives, and report on external engagement efforts to the Board.

Section 7: Duties of Communications Officer - The Communications Officer shall oversee all internal and external communications, ensuring consistency in branding and messaging. Duties include drafting and disseminating newsletters, social media content, promotional materials, and media relations in coordination with event planning and organizational initiatives.

Section 8: Duties of Social Officer - The Social Officer shall assist in advising how to bring in new members and developing strategies to maximize member retention and event attendance. The Social Officer shall coordinate closely with the Events Officer to broaden outreach and strengthen community engagement.

Section 9: Duties of Events Officer - The Events Officer shall manage the planning, logistics, and execution of both main and recruiting events. Responsibilities include coordinating with venues and vendors, developing budgets, managing event timelines, and ensuring each event aligns with the organization's objectives and standards.

Section 10: Honorary Titles - Honorary titles may be awarded to individuals at the Board's discretion.

Section 11: Contracting with Capitol Cowboys LLC - The corporation may enter into an agreement with Capitol Cowboys LLC to manage the execution, financial administration, and logistics of events, including but not limited to, the collection and disbursement of ticket revenue and expenses associated with such events. All contracts must be approved by the Board of Directors in advance. Directors of the nonprofit may not personally profit from these contracts and must recuse themselves from any vote where a conflict of interest may arise.

Section 12: Prohibition on Profit Distribution - No Director, officer, or member of the Board shall receive any share of the profits from the operations of the organization. Directors may be reimbursed only for reasonable, documented expenses incurred in service to the organization, subject to Board approval.

Section 13: Conflicts of Interest - A Director may have a financial or personal interest in a matter presented to the Board, provided that the interest is fully disclosed to the Board of Directors in advance of any vote or action. Any transaction or decision involving a potential conflict of interest must be approved by a majority of disinterested Directors acting in good faith, with reasonable inquiry and due diligence, consistent with the business judgment rule. The interested Director shall recuse themselves from both discussion and voting on the matter.

Section 14: Annual Strategic Planning - Each Officer shall submit a general plan of annual priorities and proposed executions to the Board for review and approval at the first Board meeting of their term. These plans should outline high-level goals, anticipated initiatives, and a strategy for carrying out their responsibilities effectively throughout the year. Specific details may evolve over time, but the initial framework must be in place for Board oversight and organizational alignment.

Section 15: Standing Committees - The Chair may also establish standing committees composed of Board members and general members to address specific initiatives or challenges. Such committees are valid for six months and must receive renewed approval from the Chair to continue beyond that period. The Chair may disband a committee at any time with Board approval. The purpose of standing committees is to produce a clear plan of action for Board consideration and approval.

ARTICLE VIII: Amendments

Any section of these bylaws may be amended or changed by the board by a majority vote.
